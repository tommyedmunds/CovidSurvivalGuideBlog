Covid-19 And Our Future

Preparing For A Tumultuous Future

How can young people prepare for a market that will throw them aside the moment they're inconvenient?

At the height of the pandemic, unemployment reached staggering numbers. Millions were unable to safely return to work, pay rent, or feed their families.

text

Although the economy has recovered to some degree, it's difficult for young people to feel out of danger. The dramatic roller coaster that is the Free Market can easily make another twist that sends millions more into unemployment.



Graduating College and being thrust into the "real world" seems like an insurmountable task for many. Economic recovery has been slow, but unsteady. For millions of students, the looming challenge of student loans also haunts over them. Interest on them has been delayed, but it's impossible to know when the job market will fully recover.

As we all witnessed in 2008, the housing market can be an extreme liability to the economy. Because of Covid-19, millions are unable to pay rent. And because rent forgiveness was never offered, millions of renters are sitting on months of rent piled up. Will this ticking timebomb in the real estate market explode? We can do nothing but watch and hope for the best.
