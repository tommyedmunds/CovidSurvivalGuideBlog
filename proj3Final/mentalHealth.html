Covid And Us

Mental Health During A Pandemic

As the pandemic worsened, millions felt their mental health spiral out of control. Normal coping mechanisms, like seeing friends, became impossible as the virus traveled through group after group. Many developed anxiety from constant bad news as well.

Prolonged Stress Can Cause the Following:
  • Feelings of fear, anger, sadness, worry, numbness, or frustration
  • Changes in appetite, energy, desires, and interests
  • Difficulty sleeping or nightmares
  • Difficulty concentrating and making decisions

text

Unfortunately for millions of college students, normal ways of decompressing suddenly became off-limits. For an age cohort that already is subjected to the stress of education, with exams, job hunting, and increasing tuition costs, COVID-19 felt like a punch to the gut, as many were not even sure where they could live.

Zoom calls are an important source of socialization, but even then, they are not a true substitute for real interaction with people. Many people simply find themselves unable to withstand the isolation brought about by the pandemic.

Unfortunately, there is simply no truly safe alternative to social distancing. People may find some success in hanging out outside together, although this is not an option in areas where spring has not sprung. Even then, there still may be a slight risk in catching Covid-19. As a result, our only true choice is to wait for the vaccine to fully provide herd immunity among the American population.
