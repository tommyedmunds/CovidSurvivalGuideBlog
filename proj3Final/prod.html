Faltering Productivity

Why is working from home so difficult?

Working from home seems like an ideal set-up. Why is it so difficult for so many people?

For many, working from home seemed like a dream come true. Students could simply roll out of bed and launch into their zoom classes.

text

In reality, working from home entails some unforseen consequences. Many reported feeling an unending monotony to everyday life. Learning to effectively work from home is one of the most enduring lessons of the pandemic.



Set your workdays and hours and stick to them. In most cases, that either means maintaining regular business hours or basing your work hours on the schedule maintained by your spouse or kids. Not only does a conventional schedule make you more productive, but it also allows you to spend time with the people you care about. For example, I work from around 10:30am to 2:30pm, then break for 90 minutes or so for a workout and lunch. I then return to work from around 4pm to 10pm.

Setting aside a physical workspace is extremely important as well. One of the most difficult aspects of working from home is mentally separating work from the rest of your day. Setting a hard cut-off time for you to turn off your computer can be critical in managing your time. With a set schedule, this struggle can be minimized as much as possible.
